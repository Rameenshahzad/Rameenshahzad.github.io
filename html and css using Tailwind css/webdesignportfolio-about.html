What is Automatic Changeover Switch?

It is a device that automatically transfers a power supply from its primary source to a backup source. This happen if there is a failure or outage in the primary source.
Some transfer switches are manual, in that an operator effects the transfer by throwing a switch, while others are automatic and trigger when they sense one of the sources has lost or gained power.
With an automatic changeover switch, the home's power is automatically switched over during an outage, eliminating the need for manually switching. But with a manual changeover switch, the homeowner must flip a switch to turn the power draw from the commercial grid to the generator.

Advantages and applications

Necessary for uninterrupted electrical supply
Ideal for less accessible generators
Ideal for efficiently switching to generator power
Used as a home lighting system with few modifications.
Used to drive other DC loads like a DC motor of any electronic appliance or other toy applications.
