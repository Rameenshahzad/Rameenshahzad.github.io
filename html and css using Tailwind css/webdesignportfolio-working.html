How it works

In this mode, the 555 timer IC performs the switching action by producing two types of signals:
1)Low logic signal
2)High logic signal
In this circuit, the transistor acts as the switch

Position 1

When the switch is in position 1, the reset pin is grounded. Thus, the 555 timer IC produces low logic signal.The base-emitter junction of transistor is reverse biased and in cut-off position and the LED loads are connected directly to the output of voltage regulator through the Schottky diode. AC supply is stepped down and signal is converted to unregulated fluctuating DC through the bridge rectifier.The filter capacitors remove fluctuation and DC voltage is regulated by LM 7809.

Position 2

When the switch is in position 2, the trigger pin of 555 timer IC is grounded and it produces high logic signal.The base emitter junction is forward biased so the transistor reaches saturation and is in ‘ON’ position. No current flows through Schottky diode;LEDs are connected to 9V battery through 100 Ohm resistor and BC 547 transistor.
